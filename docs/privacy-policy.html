E-Vault Password Manager — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 5, 2026

E-Vault Password Manager ("the extension", "we", "us") is a password manager that helps you securely store, autofill, capture, and update login credentials. This policy explains what data the extension handles, how it is protected, and the permissions it uses.

Zero-knowledge by design. Your master password and the encryption keys derived from it never leave your device and are never sent to our servers. All encryption and decryption happen locally in your browser. We cannot read your stored passwords.

1. Information We Handle

E-Vault Password Manager connects only to your organization's E-Vault Password Manager backend (the server you sign in to). The following data is involved:

  • Account information — the email address you sign in with, and an authentication token issued after login. Your account password is verified using cryptographic material; we do not store your master password in readable form.
  • Encrypted vault items — your saved logins (titles, usernames, passwords, URLs, notes) are encrypted on your device before being sent to the server. The server only ever stores ciphertext. Encryption keys are never transmitted.
  • Public keys & sharing metadata — to enable secure password sharing between teammates, your public key and per-item access records are stored. Shared items are re-encrypted to the recipient's public key on your device.
  • Local device storage — the extension stores your session token, a non-secret index of which sites already have saved logins (host + username only, never passwords), and your preferences (such as theme and backend environment) in the browser's extension storage. The unlocked session is kept in memory only and is cleared when you close the browser.

2. What We Do NOT Do

  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties.
  • We do not use advertising, analytics, or tracking SDKs.
  • We do not collect your browsing history. The extension only reads or writes credential fields on a page when you explicitly autofill or save a login.
  • We do not transmit data to any server other than your configured E-Vault Password Manager backend.
  • We do not include or execute any remotely-hosted code. All extension code ships inside the published package.

3. How Your Data Is Protected

  • Master key derived locally with PBKDF2-SHA256 (310,000 iterations).
  • Vault contents encrypted with AES-256-GCM.
  • Secure sharing uses RSA-OAEP 2048-bit public-key encryption.
  • Encryption keys are non-extractable and stay in your browser's secure context.
  • All network communication with the backend uses HTTPS.

4. Browser Permissions We Request

  • storage — to save your session token, preferences, and the non-secret saved-login index on your device.
  • activeTab & scripting — to fill and capture credentials on the page you are actively using, and to connect to an open, logged-in E-Vault Password Manager web-app tab.
  • clipboardWrite — to let you copy a username or password to your clipboard.
  • Host access (http://*/*, https://*/*) — like any password manager, the extension must detect login forms, autofill saved credentials, and offer to save new logins on whatever websites you choose to use. It accesses page content only to read/write credential fields when you autofill or save — it does not monitor your browsing.

5. Data Retention & Deletion

Your encrypted vault items remain on your E-Vault Password Manager backend until you (or your administrator) delete them. Removing the extension clears all locally stored tokens and preferences from your browser. To delete your account and server-side data, contact your E-Vault Password Manager administrator.

6. Children's Privacy

E-Vault Password Manager is intended for use by organizations and is not directed to children under 13.
